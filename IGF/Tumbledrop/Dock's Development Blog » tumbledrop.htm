Tumbledrop 1.1 submitted – 20 new levels!

Sunday, April 4th, 2010

I’ve just submitted Tumbledrop 1.1 to the App Store! This adds 20 new levels, and 40 new medals. It also fixes a lot of bugs, so it’s something of a milestone for me. It’s been great to have received feedback on the original release, and I’ve been working hard to make new and interesting level designs that are tricky and puzzling.

Some people have asked about iPad support, so I would like to announce that I’m working on ‘Tumbledrop Deluxe‘ coming to PC, Mac and iPad! This is an enhanced version of Tumbledrop featuring all of the levels from the iPhone, as well as new levels to take advantage of the larger screen. More details on this will come later in the month.

So, please keep an eye on the app store for Tumbledrop 1.1!

Help me fly Tumbledrop to the IGF

Wednesday, January 27th, 2010

Tumbledrop has been chosen to be finalist at the Independent Games Festival 2010. I’m really happy about this, and I’ve always dreamed of entering the competition and attending the show. As a finalist I am granted access to IGF and GDC, and I already have my ticket for these. However, I simply can’t afford the trip!  I’m a full-time indie game developer, so my income is limited.  The flight and hotel costs are way out of my budget, and right now it seems that I am unable to go… unless you help me!

  • Please donate any sum, $1 or more, to help me fly to San Francisco and take Tumbledrop to IGF.
  • Donations of $10 or more will be credited as a Patron in the next iPhone update of Tumbledrop.
  • Donations of $25 or more will be credited in bold, and also be given special thanks my next (as yet unannounced) game, due mid 2010.

I would really love to attend the Independent Game Festival and GDC in March, and I’d especially like to be meet some fellow developers in America, but without your help I won’t be able to attend.

Also, I’ve put Tumbledrop on sale for on sale for 50%, so that’s just US$0.99! I’ll keep it at this price until I’m able to afford my trip to San Francisco!  Thanks to everyone with your support, it means a great deal to me.
